GPS Goal Achievement Blueprint

1. The Goal

Launch a web app that generates R200000 per month in 6 months.

  • Timeline: 6 months (Recommended: Adjust to 12 months for increased feasibility)
  • Key Metric: Consistently earning R200000 per month from the app

2. The Why

"I want to be a millionaire."

Your drive to achieve financial freedom and reach millionaire status is the core motivation behind this goal. Keep this vision front and center as you navigate challenges along the way.

3. Anti-Goals & Constraints

  • Not affect my primary employment schedule of about 12 hours per day.

You are committed to ensuring this project does not interfere with your primary job. This boundary is non-negotiable and shapes how you allocate time and energy to your web app.

4. The Plan: Major Moves

  1. Building an MVP:

    • Develop a minimum viable product to bring your idea to life quickly and start testing with real users.

  2. Finding Paying Subscribers:

    • Go beyond user sign-ups to pursue actual revenue, focusing aggressively on converting trial or free users to paying subscribers.

  3. Retaining Subscribers:

    • Invest in customer satisfaction and product improvements to keep users coming back every month.

5. Plan Confidence & Adjustments

  • Theory Check: Adjust the goal duration from 6 to 12 months to increase achievability without compromising your employment constraints.
  • Practice Check: You rate your likelihood of following through at 85% — a solid foundation.
  • Process Tuning: Implement checklists to guarantee all key actions are taken and nothing slips through the cracks.

6. Potential Obstacles (Crystal Ball Method)

Be aware of these three major risks as you move forward:

  1. Lack of Stakeholder Buy-In:
    “If I don't get stakeholder buy-in, meaning the project gets rejected by parties who share the IP.”
  2. Delayed MVP Delivery:
    “If approved, I fail to produce the MVP in 3 months.”
  3. Failure to Acquire Target Users:
    “If approved, I fail to acquire the target users.”

Keep these front of mind and actively develop mitigation strategies as you proceed.

7. The System: Reminders, Tracking & Accountability

  • Reminders: Use calendar alerts and regular check-ins to make sure you're tackling the most important actions aligned with your objective.
  • Tracking:
    • Maintain a spreadsheet to record tasks completed.
    • Track stakeholder responses (one-time).
    • Monitor user sign-ups on a weekly basis.
  • Accountability:
    • Enforce personal accountability with creative constraints:
      • “Disallowing take-outs on Fridays.”
      • “Forfeit afternoon off from work.”
    • Consider adding social or peer accountability for even stronger follow-through.

8. Next Actions: What to Do THIS WEEK

  1. Finalize Your Plan of Action:
    Draft and begin using a weekly checklist for building your MVP, with clear, time-bound actions.

  2. Stakeholder Outreach:
    Identify key stakeholders and schedule meetings or send communications to secure their buy-in for project approval.

  3. System Setup:
    Set up your tracking spreadsheet and calendar reminders for MVP milestones, stakeholder checkpoints, and user acquisition activities.

🚀 Stay the Course!

You’ve defined a bold, financially transformative goal — and mapped clear, practical steps to achieve it, all while honoring your non-negotiables. Stick to your system, stay consistent, and revisit your “why” often. Remember: Progress is built on momentum, not perfection. Take action this week and watch the path unfold!
